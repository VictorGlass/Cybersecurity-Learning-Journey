# Business Continuity

## Overview

Business Continuity refers to the ability of an organization to continue operating during and after a disruptive event such as cyberattack, system failure, or natural disaster.

The main objetive is to minimize downtime and ensure that critical services remain available.


## Key Objetives

- Maintain essential business operations.
- Reduce operational impact.
- Protect critical assets and services.
- Ensure quick recovery after incidents.


## Business Continuity Plan (BCP)

Defines the procedures and strategies that an organization follows to maintain operations duringg incidents.


### Common Elements of a BCP

- Risk assessment.
- Recovery procedures.
- Communication plans.
- Backup strategies.
- Incident response coordination.


## Disaster Recovery (DR)

Disaster Recovery focuses on restoring systems, infrastructure, and data after a disruption.

Examples:
- Restoring backups  
- Rebuilding compromised servers  
- Recovering databases  

---

## Important Concepts

### RTO (Recovery Time Objective)

The maximum acceptable time that a system can remain unavailable.

### RPO (Recovery Point Objective)

The maximum acceptable amount of data loss measured in time.

---

## Importance of Business Continuity

- Reduces downtime  
- Improves resilience  
- Protects organizational reputation  
- Ensures operational stability  

---

## Example Scenario

A ransomware attack encrypts a company's servers.

The organization activates its Business Continuity Plan:
- Critical services are moved to backup systems  
- Data is restored from secure backups  
- Incident response teams investigate the attack
