# DFIR (Digital Forensics and Incident Response)

## Overview

DFIR combines Digital Forensics and Incident Response to investigate, contain, and recover from cybersecurity incidents.

Incident Response focuses on managing and mitigating active threats, while Digital Forensics analyzes evidence to understand what happened and identify the root cause.

---

# Incident Response Phases

## 1. Preparation

Organizations prepare policies, procedures, tools, and teams before incidents occur.

Examples:
- Security monitoring tools
- Incident response plans
- Employee training

---

## 2. Identification

The organization detects and confirms that a security incident has occurred.

Examples:
- Suspicious network activity
- Malware detection
- Unauthorized access attempts

---

## 3. Containment

The goal is to limit the spread and impact of the incident.

Examples:
- Isolating infected systems
- Blocking malicious IP addresses
- Disabling compromised accounts

---

## 4. Eradication

The root cause of the incident is removed from the environment.

Examples:
- Removing malware
- Deleting malicious files
- Patching vulnerabilities

---

## 5. Recovery

Systems are restored safely to operational status while ensuring the attacker cannot compromise them again.

Examples:
- Reinstalling compromised servers
- Restoring backups
- Integrity validation checks

---

## 6. Lessons Learned

The organization reviews the incident to improve future response capabilities.

Examples:
- Updating detection rules
- Improving security controls
- Training employees

---

# Digital Forensics

Digital Forensics investigates cybersecurity incidents by analyzing digital evidence.

Main objectives:
- Determine what happened
- Identify affected systems
- Understand attack methods
- Establish timelines
- Preserve evidence

---

# Forensic Investigation Questions

## WHAT
- What happened?
- What systems were affected?
- What was the impact?

## WHERE
- Where did the incident occur?
- Which devices or services were compromised?

## HOW
- How did the attacker gain access?
- Which tools or malware were used?

## WHO
- Who performed the attack?
- External attackers, insiders, or threat groups?

## WHEN
- When did the attack start?
- When was it detected?
- When did it end?

## WHY
- What was the attacker's motivation?
- Financial gain, espionage, hacktivism, revenge, etc.

---

# DFIR Reports

A DFIR report documents the investigation and incident response process.

Important characteristics:
- Clear and accurate
- Technically detailed
- Understandable for the target audience
- Reproducible
- Legally compliant when required

---

# Common Sections of a DFIR Report

1. Cover Page  
2. Index  
3. Executive Summary  
4. Background Information  
5. Technical Analysis  
6. Eradication and Recovery Actions  
7. Timeline  
8. Impact Assessment  
9. Attribution  
10. Conclusions  
11. Lessons Learned  
12. Appendices  

---

# Executive Summary

The executive summary is designed for management and non-technical audiences.

Characteristics:
- Clear language
- Concise information
- Main findings
- Current incident status
- Estimated impact

---

# Technical Analysis

The technical analysis section contains:
- Detailed investigation findings
- Tools and methodologies used
- Technical evidence
- Timeline analysis

This section is intended for technical professionals and incident responders.
