# Gobernance

## Overview

Governance in cybersecurity refers to the set of strategies and frameworks that an organization uses to protect itself against potential security threats and compromises.


## Core Elements

### 1. Policies

Policies define high-level objetives and security principles within an organization.

**Expample:**
Corporate accounts must use secure passwords.


### 2. Procedures

Procedures describe how policies are implemented in practice, with detailed and actionable steps.

**Example:**
In Active Directory, enforce password requirements such as:
- Minimum length greater than 12 characters.
- Use of uppercase and lowercase letters.
- Inclusion of numbers and special characters.


### 3. Standars

Standars are established best practices of framework that organizations can adopt voluntarily.

**Example:**
ISO 27001 recommends:
- Using strong and unique passwords for each account.
- Combinig letters, numbers, and symbols.


### 4. Laws and Regulations

These are mandatory requirements that organizations must comply with depending on their scope and location.

**Example:**
- ENS (Spain).
- NIS2 (European Union).

**Example requeriment:**
Minimum password length of 8 characters.


## Importance of Governance

- Establishes a structured approach to security.
- Ensures consistency across the organization.
- Helps enforce security controls.
- Reduces legall and operational risks.


## Personal Insight

Governance connects technical security measures with business and legal requirements.
It ensures that security is not only implemented, but also properly managed and enforced.
