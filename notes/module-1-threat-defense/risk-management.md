# Risk Managment 

## Overview

Risk management is the process of identifying, analyzing, and responding to potential risk that could affect an organization.

## 1. Scope Definition

Defines the boundaries of the organization or system where risk will be assessed.

## 2. Risk Assessment

Risk assessment is based on three main elements:

- **Threats** -> Potential events than can cause harm
- **Vulnerabilities** -> Weaknesses that can be exploited
- **Assets** -> What needs to be protected


## Assets

Are anything valuable to the organization:

- **Information** -> Databases, contracts, invoices
- **Software** -> Applications, development tools
- **Physical Assets** -> Laptops, servers, buildings, data centers
- **Services** -> Public websites, internal tools, Active Directory
- **People** -> Employees, managers, external users
- **Intangibles** -> Reputation and branch image


## Vulnerabilities

Are weaknesses that can be exploited by threat

### Expamples:
- Misconfigurations (firewalls, access control)
- Outdated software
- Weak or compromised ppassword
- Excessive user privileges
- Zero-day vulnerabilities


## Criticality

Risk is evaluated based on:

- **Likelihood (frequency)** -> How often it may occur
- **Impact** -> The level of damage to the organization


## 3. Risk Response

The are four main strategies:

- **Accept**
Acknowledge the risk without taking action
-> Usually requires a contingency plan

- **Avoid**
Eliminate the activity causing the risk

- **Mitigate**
Reduce the impact or likelihood
-> Example: Updating software

- **Transfer**
Shit the risk to a third party
-> Example: Cybersecurity insurance


## 4. Monitoring

Continuous evaluation of:
- Risks
- Implemented controls
- Effectiveness of responses


## Personal Insight

Understanding risk management is essential in cybersecurity.
It is not only about identifying threats, but also about making strategic decisions on how to handle them based on business impact.
